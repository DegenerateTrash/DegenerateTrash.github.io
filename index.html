Introduction

The Minimax Algorithm

Before I had even started programming 'Ur' I decided to learn the basics of the Minimax algorithm, which caused me to stumble upon the video 'Coding Challenge 154: Tic Tac Toe AI with Minimax Algorithm' by 'The Coding Train'. This video was extremely useful for learning how the Minimax algorithm worked while also providing an example of its implementation into a simple competitive game.

How It Works

As a disclaimer, I am not an expert. In fact, I only understand 40% of the Minimax algorithm as of writing so do not rely on me for information on how the Minimax algorithm functions.
As I understand it the program iterates through all possible solutions at that moment in time of which the program then assigns each outcome with a specific score. For example, in Noughts and Crosses, a winning move will be assigned a score of 1, a tie will be a score of 0 and a loss will be a score of -1. The program will then pursue an outcome with the score of one and only pursuing an outcome with a score of zero when a victory is impossible. When implemented in a simple game like Noughts and Crosses it makes the game impossible to win for the human, as the algorithm only allows for the human to achieve a loss or a tie.
